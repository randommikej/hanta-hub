The Short Answer

The first signs of hantavirus are a flu-like prodrome — fever (38–40 °C), severe muscle aches in the thighs and lower back, fatigue, and headache — appearing one to eight weeks after exposure.

The Detailed Answer

If shortness of breath develops days later, the disease has entered the cardiopulmonary phase and requires immediate emergency care. Survival depends heavily on early hospital recognition.

Sources

This answer is based on WHO Disease Outbreak News (DON599, DON600), the CDC's hantavirus clinical overview, ECDC technical assessments, and peer-reviewed Andes virus literature including the Epuyén cluster studies.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this answer specific to the 2026 MV Hondius outbreak?

It applies to both the current cluster and the broader hantavirus epidemiology. The MV Hondius cluster involves the Andes virus strain, which is the only hantavirus capable of person-to-person transmission.

Where can I read the WHO's official statements?

WHO's Disease Outbreak News DON600 (May 8) and the Director-General's May 9 message to the people of Tenerife are the most up-to-date official sources. Hanta Hub aggregates these on the main tracker.
