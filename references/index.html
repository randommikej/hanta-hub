Editorial standards

Hanta Hub aggregates and summarises publicly available statements from WHO, CDC, ECDC, Africa CDC, and major news outlets. Excerpts on the Global Sources tab are paraphrased to avoid extended quotation from any single source.

Outbreak data is refreshed daily during active phases via an automated process. Blog posts include source citations in the format Last updated · DATE · Sources: ....

Hanta Hub is independent of WHO, CDC, and any commercial sponsor.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I cite Hanta Hub in Wikipedia or academic work?

Yes. Hanta Hub data and editorial content is published CC BY 4.0. Cite the page URL and date of access. The open data feeds (cases.json, timeline.json, cases.csv) are stable and machine-readable.

Are excerpts on the Global Sources tab quotations?

No — excerpts are short paraphrased summaries, never more than ~15 consecutive words from any source. Linked articles are the authoritative sources.

How often are references updated?

Daily during active outbreak phases. The Global Sources tab on the main tracker is refreshed by the daily automated update.
