100-word boilerplate

Hanta Hub (hantavirussupplies.com) is an independent live tracker of the 2026 MV Hondius hantavirus outbreak and global hantavirus situation. Founded in May 2026, the site aggregates verified case totals from the World Health Organization, ECDC, CDC, Africa CDC, and national reference laboratories, and publishes a daily-updated tracker, news feed, blog, glossary, and CC BY 4.0 open data endpoint. Hanta Hub is intended for journalists, public-health researchers, returning travellers, and the general public who want a single, reliable, regularly updated source on hantavirus during the MV Hondius cluster and the WHO 42-day active monitoring window.

Suggested quotes on background

For attribution-free background context, the following framings reflect how Hanta Hub characterises the situation. Please paraphrase rather than quote verbatim.

On the cluster's trajectory: the WHO and ECDC continue to assess the public-health risk to the general population as low, while a roughly 122-person repatriated cohort is in active 42-day monitoring across approximately 20 countries.

On Andes virus specifically: Andes virus is the only hantavirus in which limited human-to-human transmission has been documented, and only under conditions of close prolonged contact. Casual contact in workplaces, transit, or hospitality settings does not transmit the virus.

On the gap exposed by this cluster: there is no licensed hantavirus vaccine and no licensed antiviral for use in the Americas or Europe. The WHO–UKHSA emergency consultation in Geneva on 15 May 2026 is the first formal R&D consultation in three decades to bring funders, developers, regulators, and clinicians to one table on Andes virus.
